SECTION 1 | about

The Amino Blogging Assistant is designed for bloggers on the Amino Apps mobile app to stylise their blogs with unprecedented ease.

In every community, you will find stunning blogs with eye-catching, evenly-divided paragraphs and beautiful aesthetics.

But realise that users that do spend the time to make their blogs look so polished also end up spending hours, if not days, adjusting the margins, formatting, and spacing of their paragraphs.

Why does it take so much effort?

The Amino blog editor is a plain-text (as opposed to a rich-text) editor. All the formatting is done character-by-character, line-by-line, with no shortcuts in-between. This means that those fancy indentations and spaces and lines that we see had been inputted manually (and painstakingly) by the user, with each style replicated on every single new line of text (rather than the formatting being on one paragraph).

With the Amino Blogging Assistant, you can now format your entire blog paragraph with the push of a button. No longer will you need to spend hours breaking the 'Space' button to indent your text or squint your eyes to see if the text is lined-up. Simply select the formatting that you want for each line, and the Amino Blogging Assistant utility will automatically generate all the text formatting for you in seconds.

SECTION 2 | step-by-step tutorial

step 1: text alignment

The first step is to select your text alignment: either left or center alignment. 'Left-alignment' is set by default. If you select 'Center-alignment', your text style will automatically generate the [C] tag attached to it.

step 2: text styles

The second step is to select from the four available text styles: italic, bold, underline, and strikethrough. In the Amino blog editor, these four styles correspond to the following tags: [I], [B], [U], and [S] respectively. You can check multiple boxes for multiple styles. If you do not want any text styles, leave all the checkboxes empty.

step 3: indentation

Here, you can specify the number of spaces that you want before your text as indentation. The spaces will appear after the text style tag but before the custom starter (see Step 4) and the actual line of text. If you do not want any space indentation, leave the value at 0. Non-integer values are invalid.

step 4: custom starting characters

In this step, you can specify any characters or symbols (including spaces) that you want to appear after the space indentation but before the actual line of text.

For example, you can enter '| ' (including the ending space) to generate this aesthetic effect with your paragraph:

| Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet,
| meis sadipscing at mea. Cu eum
| falli saperet, cu vel suas
| magna solum, vix hinc posse
| omnesque te.

This feature supports special, non-keyboard symbols as well. If you do not want starting characters, leave this field blank.

line style preview

This line preview shows you what your line style will look like with your current settings. This updates immediately as you change the settings so you can see how your text will be formatted beforehand. If something looks amiss, you can return to the settings above and make any revisions accordingly until you are satisfied. Please note that you cannot type directly into that box; it is only a preview.

step 5: maximum line characters

Here, you can specify the maximum number of characters that you want per line. The Amino Blog Assistant will divide up your paragraph for you based on that maximum character count. The minimum value is 15 characters per line. 30 - 50 characters per line is recommended. Non-integer values are invalid.

step 6: your paragraph

Enter your paragraph text into the box on one line. Then, press the 'Format text' button. Only one paragraph can be formatted at a time. Multiple paragraphs are currently not supported; the program may not run properly if you attempt to format more than one paragraph.

step 7: your formatted text

After pressing the 'Format text' button, the Amino Blogging Assistant will generate your formatted text according to your style settings. If you wish to revise your settings, just make the changes above and then press the 'Format text' button again to update your text formatting.

step 8: copy and paste into amino

Once you are satisfied, you can copy your formatted text and paste it directly into the Amino App blog editor.

SECTION 3 | tips and tricks

1 | fancy fonts

Like to use fancy fonts? Those work here, too. You can copy and paste your paragraph text into font generators such as Cool Fancy Text Generator or Fancy Letters. However, using the Stylish Text app may cause the program to crash.

2 | custom start as fancy indentation

If you don't want to settle for the plain indentation, leave 'Indentation' at 0 and customise your entire line style in the 'Custom Starting Characters' field (including spaces), and you can create some very unique text designs. You can copy and paste special, non-keyboard symbols from websites such as Cool Symbol.
